Force is a push or a pull that causes an object to change its state of motion or shape. It is a vector quantity, which means it has both magnitude (amount of force) and direction. Forces can be classified into two main categories: contact forces and non-contact forces.

Contact forces are those that arise from physical contact between objects, such as:

1. Frictional force: opposes motion between two surfaces in contact
2. Normal force: perpendicular force exerted by a surface on an object in contact with it
3. Tension force: force exerted by a stretched string or rope
4. Applied force: force exerted by an object on another object, such as a push or a pull

Non-contact forces are those that act between objects without physical contact, such as:

1. Gravitational force: attractive force between two objects with mass
2. Electromagnetic force: force between charged particles, such as protons and electrons
3. Magnetic force: force between magnetic fields and moving charges or other magnetic fields

The unit of force is typically measured in Newtons (N), which is defined as the force required to accelerate a 1-kilogram object by 1 meter per second squared.

Force is an important concept in physics and engineering, as it is used to describe and predict the motion of objects, the behavior of materials, and the design of structures and machines. The study of forces is a fundamental aspect of understanding the natural world and is crucial for making technological advances.
